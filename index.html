  • June funnel costs are under target — Cost/MQL $329 vs ~$500, Cost/SQL $439 vs ~$1,000 — and Cost/MQL has fallen four months straight ($868 → $329). Definitions per the sheet: MQL = qualified for a discovery call, SQL = deal created (~2 MQLs → 1 SQL); the ~$4,000 CAC target assumes a 25% close rate on SQLs.
  • The counting method changed mid-stream: March–May MQL counts (101–127/mo) are the old HubSpot-derived numbers; from ~June, MQLs and SQLs are marked manually per lead in the sheet's Inbound Leads tab under a much stricter bar (4 MQLs, 1 SQL marked among all paid leads to date). Don't compare across that boundary — and with counts this small, unit costs swing on single deals.2
  • CAC = the month's ad spend ÷ that month's closed-won deals — months with zero closed-won have no CAC (the sheet's modeled "CPA" column is excluded; it implied customers that didn't exist). The first closed-won is attributed to May, its lead month: Rohrer Manufacturing, the $280k job (die-casting lead May 13, MQL → SQL → closed).6 May CAC = $4,833.80 ÷ 1 ≈ $4,834, just above the ~$4,000 target — while the deal itself returned ~15× the entire four-month ad budget ($280k vs $18,531 total spend).
